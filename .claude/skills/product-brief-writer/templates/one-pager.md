# <Product name> — Product Brief

**Owner:** <name> · **Status:** Draft v0.1 · **Date:** <YYYY-MM-DD>

## Pitch

> One paragraph (2–4 sentences). What is it, who is it for, and what changes for them. The reader should be able to repeat this paragraph back to a colleague without re-reading.

## Problem

- What pain or opportunity exists today, in concrete terms.
- What users do *now* instead (the workaround they live with — spreadsheet, second app, asking a colleague, doing without).
- Why this matters enough to fix: frequency, severity, business impact.

## Target user

- One sentence describing the *wedge* user — the first 100 people who will care most.
- Behavior, role, or context (not demographics). Example: "Solo gym-goers who already track workouts in a notes app and want progressive overload across multiple gyms."

## Value proposition

- One sentence: what changes for the target user when they have this.
- Optional: a specific before/after ("Onboarding drops from ~45 min to under 10 min for first-time SMB owners").

## Shape of the solution

3–5 bullets describing the *kind* of solution we're betting on — enough to make the value prop credible, not a feature list.

- <Bullet 1 — a defining mechanic, surface, or approach>
- <Bullet 2 — what makes it different from the obvious alternative>
- <Bullet 3 — an opinionated choice that anchors the user experience>

*Feature-level detail (what fields, what flows, what edge cases) belongs in the PRD — not here.*

## Non-goals

Specific things people will reflexively ask for that we are choosing NOT to do (yet). Each one with a reason.

- <Thing 1> — *because <reason / what we'd need to know first / why it's lower priority>*
- <Thing 2> — *because <reason>*

## Success metrics

What we'll measure to know if this is working. Each metric must be measurable and time-bound.

- <Metric 1>: from <baseline or "TBD"> to <target> within <timeframe>
- <Metric 2>: from <baseline or "TBD"> to <target> within <timeframe>

## Top risk *(optional)*

The single risk most likely to kill this — what it is, and the cheapest experiment to de-risk it.

## Timing intent *(optional)*

1–2 sentences only — and only if timing is a *constraint on the decision*. Example: "Want to validate before Q3 buying cycle starts in August." Detailed milestones with dates belong in the project plan, not the brief.

---

*This is a one-pager brief. If we move forward, the next document is a PRD (or design spike) — not a longer brief. Dated milestones belong in a separate project plan.*
